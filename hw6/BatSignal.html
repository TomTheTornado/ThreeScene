  • Camera Controls
  • w, a, s, d - move forward, left, back, right
  • r, f - move up, down
  • i, j, k, l - look up, left, down, right
  • I, J, K, L - orbit down, right, up, left
  • O - face origin
  • p - put camera at origin
  • W - decrease fov
  • S - increase fov

  • t, T - Turn the bat signal
  • u, U - Angle the bat signal

  • 1, 2 - Change the skybox

  • x, y, z - Change the Batman logo to rotate around the x, y, and z, axes, respectively
  • 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 - Change the background color behind the Batman logo

  • Started with "../threejsexamples/RotatingCubeWithModel.js", I used the camera controls from the examples. I added 2 variations of a skybox, a snow skybox, and a space skybox. After adding all of that I decided on making the bat signal. I found the batman symbol on thingiverse and converted it to and obj and unwrapped its coordinates in blender. I modeled the batman logo in blender by converting an .svg to a mesh and extruding it. I used heirarchy to create the bat signal that way it could be adjusted easily. I added metal textures to the different objects as well. I decided to render the Batman '66 logo in the framebuffer and give the ability to change the background colors of it. I added more spotlights around the bat signal as well, this time showing off different colors of the light.
